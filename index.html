TMT Quenching System

General Arrangement

The TMT box comprises three lines: the first for 10 mm / 12 mm, the second for 16 mm / 20 mm, and the third for 25 mm / 32 mm. It has an overall length of 15.3 m and a width of 0.96 m.

10 MM / 12 MM Line

There are two lines of equal length for slit rolling. Each line contains 12 pipes with nozzles cum water boxes and 3 pipes without nozzles. The diameter of each line is 22 mm. The arrangement of each line is identical and as follows:

  • 3 Pipes (Length of Pipe 1: 760 mm; Pipes 2 & 3: 470 mm)
  • 3 TMT pipes of each 1800 mm in length
    Pipes 1 & 2 are cooled externally by a water shower to avoid heat damage, as they run without water for approximately 6000 mm to control the yield strength of the bar.
  • 4 TMT pipes of each 795 mm in length
  • 5 TMT nozzle-less pipes of each 295 mm in length
  • 3 Water strippers of each 150 mm in length
  • 1 Air stripper

16 MM / 20 MM Line

There is 1 pipe without nozzles and 9 pipes with nozzles and water boxes. The diameter of the line is 35 mm. The arrangement and lengths are as follows:

  • 1 Pipe with nozzles of 900 mm
  • 2 TMT pipes of each 2850 mm
  • 7 TMT pipes of each 795 mm
  • 3 Water strippers of each 150 mm
  • 1 Air stripper

25 MM / 32 MM Line

There are 6 pipes with nozzles and water boxes. The diameter of the line is 45 mm. The arrangement and lengths are as follows:

  • 1 TMT pipe of 3235 mm (an empty pipe is attached in front of the water box with flange)
  • 5 TMT pipes of each 1510 mm
  • 3 Water strippers of each 150 mm
  • 1 Air stripper

Water Circuit

The 10â€ (Schedule 40) water header is connected to the TMT lines through a series of pipelines:

  • 2â€ lines for 10 mm / 12 mm
  • 4â€ lines for 16 mm / 20 mm / 25 mm / 32 mm
Each pipeline has individual flow control valves. The pipelines are distinctly colored and numbered for easy identification.

Natural Gas Energy Accounting

Unlike many commodities, natural gas is not usually traded by volume but by the energy released during combustion. For operational control and fuel efficiency monitoring, engineers must convert gas consumption into energy units, typically MMBTU.

Calorific Value (CV) of Natural Gas

The calorific value defines the amount of energy released when a unit volume of gas is completely combusted.

\[ \text{CV} = 1026.9 \; \text{BTU/SCF} \]
  • BTU â€“ British Thermal Unit
  • SCF â€“ Standard Cubic Foot (at standard conditions)

Gas Consumption in MSCM

In plant operation, natural gas flow is generally recorded in MSCM (Thousand Standard Cubic Meters).

Example:
Gas consumption = 60.878 MSCM

Unit Conversion: MSCM â†’ MMSCF

For energy calculations, gas volume must be expressed in MMSCF (Million Standard Cubic Feet).

\[ 1 \; \text{MSCM} = 0.0353147 \; \text{MMSCF} \]
\[ 60.878 \times 0.0353147 = 2.15 \; \text{MMSCF} \]

Energy Calculation

The total energy input from natural gas is calculated as:

\[ \text{Energy (MMBTU)} = \text{Gas Consumption (MMSCF)} \times \text{CV (BTU/SCF)} \]

Substituting values:

\[ 2.15 \times 1026.9 = 2208 \; \text{MMBTU} \]
Always express final gas consumption in MMBTU to maintain uniformity in energy accounting and for meaningful furnace performance monitoring.
